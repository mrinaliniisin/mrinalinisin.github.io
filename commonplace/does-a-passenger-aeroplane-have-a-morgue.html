Does a passenger aeroplane have a morgue?

No — commercial passenger flights don't have a dedicated morgue. If someone dies in flight, the body is typically moved to an empty seat or row when available and covered with a blanket; otherwise it stays in the person's own seat, handled as dignifiedly as space allows. One historical exception: some long-haul aircraft, such as the Singapore Airlines Airbus A340-500, had a special body compartment — sometimes nicknamed a “corpse cupboard” — fitted with straps and used only when no other suitable space was available. But that was a rare feature, not a standard morgue.
