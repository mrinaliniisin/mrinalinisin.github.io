Why Logarithms?

Logarithms are used to solve exponential equations, calculate compound interest, and measure quantities that span a very large range of values on a logarithmic scale — like sound intensity (decibels) or the strength of an earthquake (Richter scale). They're also useful across computer science, finance, chemistry, and physics for analysing growth rates, data compression, and radioactive decay. A few uses: solving exponential equations (logs are the inverse of exponents, so they find unknown exponents — e.g. how long an investment takes to reach a certain value); compressing wide ranges (on the decibel scale a 10 dB increase is 10× louder and 20 dB is 100×; the Richter scale measures earthquake magnitude); modelling growth and decay (population growth, radiocarbon dating); and in computing, data compression and information theory (the number of bits needed to store a value).
