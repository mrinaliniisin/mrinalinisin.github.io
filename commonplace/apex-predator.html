Apex predator

An apex predator is a carnivore at the top of its food chain, with no natural predators as an adult, that controls prey populations and plays a crucial role in maintaining ecosystem balance. They occupy the highest trophic level, with examples like lions, orcas, great white sharks, and polar bears. By regulating prey numbers, they prevent overgrazing and maintain the health of other species, preventing ecosystem collapse. A classic example: when wolves (apex predators) were removed from Yellowstone, elk populations exploded, leading to overgrazing of willow and aspen trees; reintroducing wolves helped restore balance by controlling elk numbers.
