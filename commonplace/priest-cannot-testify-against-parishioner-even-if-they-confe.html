Priest cannot testify against parishioner even if they confessed to a crime such as homicide in USA and India

PENDING_TRANSCRIPTION_PLUS: In India, a confession made to a priest in a confessional setting is generally NOT admissible in an Indian court, as it is considered an extra-judicial confession, which is weak evidence requiring corroboration. It is also likely to be considered voluntary unless made under duress or inducement; the court weighs the credibility of the confession and whether the priest's testimony is reliable. Why it is not admissible: extra-judicial confession (made outside a judicial proceeding); under the Indian Evidence Act a confession is only admissible if voluntary and not made under threat, inducement, or promise; extra-judicial confessions are generally considered weak and require independent corroborating evidence. It could potentially be used as corroborating evidence if supported by independent reliable evidence, but if made under duress, or if the priest cannot provide corroboration, it will not be admitted.
