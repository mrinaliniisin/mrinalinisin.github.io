First Dibs

"First dibs" means to claim the first right or choice to something before anyone else, originating from a 17th-century English children's game called "dibstones," where players used sheep knuckle-bones (dibs) to call a share; the term evolved into American slang for claiming priority, becoming popular in the 1900s. "Dibs" as a share: in the early 1800s, "dibs" also meant a portion or share, possibly related to the game, as seen in old poems where people would "make Nunky surrender his dibs" (money/share). The phrase "calling dibs" became common in the U.S. by the early 20th century.
