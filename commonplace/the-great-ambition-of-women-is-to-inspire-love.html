The great ambition of women is to inspire love

"The great ambition of women is to inspire love" is a famous quote by the French playwright Moliere (Jean-Baptiste Poquelin). The original French line, "La grande ambition des femmes est d'inspirer l'amour," is often cited to reflect 17th-century views on gender, romance, and societal roles. It is famously used by the character Hercule Poirot in the 1978 film adaptation of Agatha Christie's Death on the Nile.
