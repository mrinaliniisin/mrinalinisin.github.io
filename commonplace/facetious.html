Facetious

Facetious (adjective): treating serious issues with deliberately inappropriate humour; flippant -- "a facetious remark." Derivatives: facetiously (adverb), facetiousness (noun). Origin: mid 16th century (in facetiousness in the general sense 'wittiness'): from French facetieux, from facetie, from Latin facetia 'jest', from facetus 'witty'.

Source: Oxford Dictionary (Apple Dictionary)
