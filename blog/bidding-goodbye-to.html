Bidding Goodbye to…

June 26, 2026

For as long as I can remember I have always used a head pillow and a pillow to hug when sleeping. Be it night sleep or naps. In the past few months, I found myself flipping the head pillow to the lower side. My pillow has a raised side and a not-so-raised side. The lower side felt better. Recently, the lower side also left me with a stiff neck in the morning. I discovered, I no longer need a pillow. WTH?

The answer, it turns out, is pole fitness and cross-training.

Before I started training, my shoulders were rounded and my head sat slightly forward — that classic desk-person posture. The pillow wasn't just comfort, it was compensation. It was shimming a wobbly table: propping my head into a neutral position because my baseline wasn't neutral.

Training changed my baseline. My shoulders retracted, my head stacked back over my spine. Now when I sleep on a pillow, it pushes my head forward — recreating the very misalignment my body has spent months correcting. My neck muscles, which have learned to hold a new position, spend all night on a passive stretch they didn't ask for. Hence the stiff neck.

The pillow that used to fix the problem became the problem.

No pillow, extra-firm mattress, neck happy. Funny how fixing the root cause makes the workaround obsolete.
